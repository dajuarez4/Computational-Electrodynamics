\QuadBox{(TL)}{QCBlue}{1) Expansion Structure}{
For $r\gg r'$:
\[
\Phi(\mathbf r)=\frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\int \frac{\rho(\mathbf r')}{|\mathbf r-\mathbf r'|}\,d^3r'
\]
\[
\Phi=\Phi_{\text{mono}}+\Phi_{\text{dip}}+\Phi_{\text{quad}}+\cdots
\]
Small parameter:
\[
\frac{r'}{r}\ll 1
\]
Monopole scales as $1/r$, dipole as $1/r^2$, quadrupole as $1/r^3$.
}
\QuadBox{(TR)}{QCGreen}{2) Moments}{
Total charge:
\[
Q=\int \rho(\mathbf r')\,d^3r'
\]
Dipole moment:
\[
\mathbf p=\int \mathbf r'\rho(\mathbf r')\,d^3r'
\]
Dipole potential:
\[
\Phi_{\text{dip}}=\frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{\mathbf p\cdot \hat{\mathbf r}}{r^2}
\]
Quadrupole tensor:
\[
Q_{ij}=\int \rho(\mathbf r')\left(3x_i'x_j'-r'^2\delta_{ij}\right)d^3r'
\]
}
\QuadBox{(BL)}{QCOrange}{3) Symmetry Rules}{
Spherical symmetry:

Only monopole survives outside.

Neutral distribution:
\[
Q=0 \Longrightarrow \text{monopole vanishes}
\]
Inversion symmetry often kills odd moments.

Reflection symmetry can force components of $\mathbf p$ to vanish.

Axial symmetry usually leads to Legendre-polynomial angular structure.
}
\QuadBox{(BR)}{QCPurple}{4) Useful Results and Checks}{
Ideal dipole potential:
\[
\Phi=\frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{\mathbf p\cdot \mathbf r}{r^3}
\]
Ideal dipole field:
\[
\mathbf E=\frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{1}{r^3}\left[3(\mathbf p\cdot \hat{\mathbf r})\hat{\mathbf r}-\mathbf p\right]
\]
\textbf{Checks}

1. Keep origin dependence in mind.

2. If $Q\neq0$, dipole moment depends on chosen origin.

3. Multipole truncations fail near or inside the source region.
}
