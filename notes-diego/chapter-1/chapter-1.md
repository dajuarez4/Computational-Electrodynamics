# Chapter 1

These notes are basically my way of slowing Jackson down a little bit and rewriting the chapter in a way that I can actually study from later without feeling lost after two pages.

I do not want this to read like a polished textbook. I want it to feel like the kind of notes I would keep next to me while working problems: direct, a little informal, and focused on what actually matters.

So the point here is:

- keep the main ideas clear
- write the derivations in a way that still makes sense when I come back later
- connect the math to the physical picture
- leave myself something more useful than just highlighting the book

This chapter is the starting point for electrostatics, so I want to use it to build the language correctly from the beginning instead of memorizing formulas without context.

I will keep the jackson notation as that was something I realized was a problem for me the change of notation in every book or paper.
